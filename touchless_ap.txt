Touchless AP: The End of Manual Invoice Processing

An alternative that involves less manual processing than before

The principle behind this alternative is the following: the invoice arrives, it gets analyzed, cross-verified with the purchase order and delivery notice, and approved for payment if everything is correct. There will be no need for manual analysis; finance professionals will only become involved in case some issues arise.

This means much less manual input from your side, reduced email traffic, and almost no time wasted trying to reconcile data between different documents.

All these benefits are available thanks to Touchless AP technology, which aims to make the whole invoice processing faster and more efficient.

Why Is This Still Relevant for Your Finance Team?

Processing one invoice may still take a lot of time for many companies, although it does not usually involve complicated decisions about whether to pay or not. Most of the issues are connected with too many manual operations, scattered across several tools, and numerous verifications.

There is still a big number of finance teams who rely predominantly on manual invoicing and data processing. Invoices get entered manually, there are many discrepancies, and AP professionals may end up having to devote most of their time to solving exceptions rather than doing valuable work.

It results in such typical problems as delayed payments, unnecessary penalties and interest, inability to get any discounts, bad relations with suppliers, and too much time devoted to routine rather than improvement.

All the above issues come out of the fundamental operation of invoice processing: verification of purchase orders, goods receipt and invoices from suppliers.

Touchless AP Workflow

Touchless AP is an automated solution driven by AI to match three-party documents related to purchase orders, goods receipt notes, and supplier invoices with minimal involvement of humans involved.

The workflow includes collecting data from the invoices, validating them against the source documents, applying tolerance thresholds, and forwarding only the real issues to the humans involved for resolving. In simple cases, there will be no delays or human interventions since the invoice will flow smoothly through the system.

The purpose is quite obvious – let the system take care of routine things and humans focus on solving exceptions.

Three Source Records for Approval of Any Invoice

On a basic level, the accounts payable tries to resolve one single issue. It has to check whether it is worth paying a particular invoice.

For this reason, there are three source documents to analyze during each invoice approval process. The Purchase Order provides details about items to buy and their prices. The Goods Receipt Note specifies the goods actually received from suppliers. The Supplier Invoice indicates the amount required to pay.

If all three documents match, then no problem with approving such an invoice occurs. However, any discrepancy requires analyzing the reason behind it. That's why most of the time the invoice approval process stalls at this point.

How It Works

Step 1 - The Invoice Arrives – A vendor sends the invoice via email. The system automatically detects it so that no one needs to upload it manually.

Step 2 - The Invoice is Extracted & Structured – The process identifies relevant invoice data elements such as line items, quantities, prices, taxes, and sums so they can be validated later on.

Step 3 - The system verifies the match – The invoice data is compared against purchase orders and goods received data in the transaction system.

Step 4 - If the match happens, it's all good – When all the differences are within predefined tolerance levels, the invoice status will be matched with details, and the AP can approve it right away.

Step 5 - Otherwise, it's sent to the appropriate person – If something seems wrong, the system highlights the problem, explains what caused it, and forwards it to the relevant party to take action.
